THREAT INTELLIGENCE REPORT — Salt Typhoon (Earth Estries / GhostEmperor)
Source: US Government / CISA / FBI Joint Advisory, December 2024
Classification: TLP:WHITE (public disclosure)

ACTOR OVERVIEW
==============
Salt Typhoon (also tracked as Earth Estries, FamousSparrow, GhostEmperor, UNC2286)
is a Chinese state-sponsored threat actor assessed to be affiliated with China's
Ministry of State Security (MSS). The group conducted one of the most significant
telecommunications espionage campaigns ever disclosed against the United States,
compromising at least eight major US telecom providers and dozens of countries' carriers.

The primary objective was intelligence collection: the actor accessed lawful intercept
(CALEA) systems used by the FBI and other federal agencies, and monitored the
communications of senior US government officials, political figures, and individuals
of intelligence interest to China.

INITIAL ACCESS
==============
Multiple initial access vectors were observed across victims:

1. Exploitation of Ivanti Connect Secure VPN appliances:
   CVE-2023-46805 (authentication bypass, CVSS 8.2)
   CVE-2024-21887 (command injection, CVSS 9.1)
   CVE-2024-21893 (SSRF, CVSS 8.2)
   These were chained for unauthenticated remote code execution.

2. Exploitation of Cisco IOS XE:
   CVE-2023-20198 (privilege escalation, CVSS 10.0)
   CVE-2023-20273 (web UI command injection, CVSS 7.2)
   Used to implant backdoors on internet-facing Cisco infrastructure.

3. Exploitation of unpatched Microsoft Exchange servers:
   ProxyLogon / ProxyShell chains in older Exchange deployments.

TARGET: LAWFUL INTERCEPT SYSTEMS
==================================
The most significant aspect of this campaign was actor access to carrier
CALEA (Communications Assistance for Law Enforcement Act) infrastructure.
CALEA systems are legally mandated backdoors in US telecom networks that allow
authorized law enforcement to intercept communications with a court order.

The actor accessed these systems to:
  - Identify individuals under active US federal surveillance
  - Monitor voice, SMS, and metadata for targets of interest
  - Potentially identify US intelligence sources and methods

This represents a severe counterintelligence impact.

C2 INFRASTRUCTURE
=================
The actor used a multi-hop relay network:
  - 27.102.113.57   (confirmed C2, Hong Kong-based VPS)
  - 43.254.132.88   (C2 relay, Singapore)
  - 103.27.108.44   (C2 relay, Malaysia)
  - 167.88.177.151  (exfiltration endpoint, Netherlands)

C2 domains:
  update.windowsdefender-telemetry.com
  cdn.microsoft-security-patch.net
  telemetry.office365-cdn.org

All domains use Let's Encrypt certificates and mimic Microsoft service names
to blend with legitimate network traffic.

PERSISTENCE AND LATERAL MOVEMENT
==================================
  - Deployed web shells on Exchange and IIS servers for persistent access:
    SHA256 of ASPX shell: 7c9e1b3d5f7a9c1e3b5d7f9a1c3e5b7d9f1a3c5e7b9d1f3a5c7e9b1d3f5a7c9
  - Used compromised admin credentials to authenticate to telco management systems
  - Moved laterally via legitimate telco management protocols (SNMP, SS7, RADIUS)
  - Accessed network tap infrastructure used for lawful intercept
  - Maintained persistence for 1–3 years undetected at some victims

EXFILTRATION
============
  - Targeted data: call records, SMS metadata, voice intercept audio
  - Volume: tens of millions of call records from multiple carriers
  - Method: compressed archives exfiltrated via C2 channel
  - Secondary path: direct operator-level access to telco infrastructure
    bypassed need for traditional exfil — data accessed in-place

DETECTION INDICATORS
======================
Process/command indicators:
  - ntdsutil.exe usage on non-DC systems
  - wmic.exe spawning unusual child processes on servers
  - Unexpected PowerShell remoting (WinRM) to telecom management hosts
  - IIS/Exchange worker process spawning cmd.exe or PowerShell

Network indicators:
  - Outbound HTTPS to domains registered within 30 days
  - Long-duration low-bandwidth beacon connections (60–300 second intervals)
  - SNMP and RADIUS authentication from unexpected source hosts

MITIGATIONS
===========
  - Emergency patch: Ivanti CVE-2023-46805, CVE-2024-21887, CVE-2024-21893
  - Emergency patch: Cisco IOS XE CVE-2023-20198, CVE-2023-20273
  - Audit all accounts with access to CALEA / lawful intercept systems
  - Segment carrier management networks from corporate IT
  - Deploy integrity monitoring on Exchange / IIS web roots
  - Implement outbound TLS inspection on carrier management networks
  - Review all service accounts for unexpected authentication patterns

UNCERTAINTIES
=============
  - Full scope of victim carriers has not been publicly disclosed; likely understated
  - GhostSpider modular architecture means observed capabilities may not reflect full toolkit
  - Actor may retain access via undisclosed persistence mechanisms
  - Impact to ongoing law enforcement operations has not been publicly assessed
  - Attribution is high-confidence but MSS unit affiliation not confirmed publicly
